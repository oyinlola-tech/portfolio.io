The Pros and Cons of AI in Education (ChatGPT, etc.)

Balanced look at practical uses, risks, and how schools can adopt AI responsibly.

AI offers powerful supports â€” personalized explanations, quick feedback, and content generation â€” but it also raises concerns about academic integrity, bias, and over-reliance. The key is using AI as an assistant, not a replacement for learning processes.

Potential benefits

  • Personalized practice and immediate explanations.
  • Time-savers for teachers (drafting, formative feedback).
  • Accessibility tools: summarization, translation, scaffolds.

Key risks

  • Academic dishonesty if students submit AI-generated work without attribution.
  • Incorrect or biased outputs; requires human verification.
  • Risk of over-dependence, reducing effortful practice.

Practical classroom guidelines

  1. Teach responsible use: require drafts, show process, and credit AI assistance.
  2. Use AI for feedback and differentiation, not as the final evaluator.
  3. Model source-checking: show how to verify AI claims.

Takeaway

AI can amplify good teaching when used transparently, with guardrails and a focus on learning processes. Train students to use AI critically, not blindly.
