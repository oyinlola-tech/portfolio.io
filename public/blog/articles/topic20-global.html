Global Education: What Can We Learn from School Systems in Other Countries?

Short comparisons of practices in Finland, Singapore, and Estonia, with practical takeaways.

High-performing systems share features: strong teacher preparation, coherent curricula, and emphasis on equity. Here are highlights from three notable systems and practical ideas that can be adapted locally.

Finland

Focus: teacher professionalism, play-based early years, low-stakes assessment. Takeaway: trust teachers and reduce unnecessary testing early on.

Singapore

Focus: curriculum coherence, emphasis on deep understanding in math, strong teacher training. Takeaway: clarity in learning progressions and targeted professional development.

Estonia

Focus: early digital literacy, teacher autonomy, attention to equity. Takeaway: prioritize digital skills and policies that support equal access.

Common themes and how to apply them

  • Invest in teacher development and career pathways.
  • Design coherent standards and align assessment to those standards.
  • Focus on equity: targeted supports ensure all students can access learning.

Takeaway

No system is a perfect transplant, but many practices are adaptable: invest in teachers, align curriculum and assessment, and prioritize equitable access to learning.
