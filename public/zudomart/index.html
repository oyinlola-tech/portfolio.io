Press Release Overview

Africa's informal peer-to-peer economy supports millions of microentrepreneurs but remains fragmented and vulnerable to fraud. An estimated 65% of traders report losses to scams on informal platforms; Nigeria alone represents a sizeable portion of the continent's ecommerce activity. Investors and ecosystem actors are prioritizing digital trust, local payments, and creator monetization tools that adapt to African realities.

What ZudoMart Delivers

ZudoMart is an African-first superapp built to empower entrepreneurs with an integrated marketplace for goods, services and micro-gigs, live commerce features, and a multi-layer trust engine. Core product highlights include:

  • Six-tier verification system with AI fraud detection
  • Escrow payments for secure transactions
  • Hyperlocal discovery (campus and neighborhood filters)
  • ZudoReels short video content for product showcasing
  • Live stream monetization with virtual gifts
8.1M Active ecommerce traders today
12M+ Target users by 2025

ZudoMart targets 8.1 million active ecommerce traders today, with plans to expand to 12M+ users across the continent by 2025.

Market Opportunity

Why Now?

Mobile adoption, local mobile money ecosystems, and rising creator economies have created a window to migrate informal transactions onto a secure, purpose-built platform. Growing advertiser interest in GenZ audiences and demand for affordable microloan and payout integrations further accelerate timing for a trust-first marketplace tailored to African use cases.

Competitive Landscape

ZudoMart addresses gaps across three categories:

  • General social platforms (where scams proliferate in DMs)
  • Classifieds (limited dispute resolution)
  • Global freelance marketplaces (high fees and poor fit for micro-gigs)

ZudoMart's differentiators are escrow for small transactions, localized payment options, campus-level discovery, and built-in creator monetization tools.
